## üè¢ Business Context
TeleConnect operates in a highly competitive telecommunications market where customer switching costs are low and retention is critical. The company currently faces:
- A churn rate of approximately **26.5%**, higher than industry benchmarks  
- Significant monthly and annual revenue loss due to churn  
- High customer acquisition costs  
- Reactive retention efforts with limited segmentation  

This milestone aims to provide a **data-driven understanding of churn behavior** to support proactive retention initiatives.

## üîç Key Analyses Performed

### 1Ô∏è‚É£ Overall Churn Rate & Revenue Impact
- Total customers analyzed: **7,043**
- Churned customers: **1,869**
- Overall churn rate: **~26.5%**
- Churn contributes to significant monthly and annual revenue loss, highlighting the financial urgency of churn reduction.

---

### 2Ô∏è‚É£ Demographic Segmentation
- **Gender:** Minimal difference in churn rates between male and female customers  
- **Senior Citizens:** Significantly higher churn (~41.7%) compared to non-senior customers  
- **Family Status:**  
  - Single customers show the highest churn  
  - Customers with dependents show the lowest churn  

**Insight:** Life-stage factors influence churn more strongly than gender.

---

### 3Ô∏è‚É£ Service Usage Analysis
- Core connectivity services (phone, internet, multiple lines) show moderate to high churn  
- Entertainment services (streaming TV/movies) do not significantly reduce churn  
- Value-added services (Online Security, Tech Support, Online Backup, Device Protection) are associated with **lower churn rates**

**Insight:** Support and protection services increase perceived value and customer stickiness.

---

### 4Ô∏è‚É£ Contract & Payment Method Analysis
- **Month-to-month contracts** exhibit the highest churn (~42.7%)  
- **Two-year contracts** show minimal churn (~2.8%)  
- Manual payment methods (electronic and mailed checks) are linked to higher churn compared to automatic payments  

**Insight:** Commitment and reduced payment friction significantly improve retention.

---

### 5Ô∏è‚É£ Tenure Cohort Analysis
- Highest churn occurs in the **first 12 months** (~47.4%)  
- Churn steadily declines with increased tenure  
- Long-tenure customers (49+ months) show strong loyalty  

**Insight:** Early customer lifecycle is the most critical retention window.

---

### 6Ô∏è‚É£ Pricing & Spend Analysis
- Higher monthly charges correlate with increased churn  
- Low total spend customers churn more frequently  
- Indicates a **price‚Äìvalue mismatch**, not price alone, as a churn driver  

---

## üß† Key Churn Patterns Identified
- High churn among month-to-month contract customers  
- Elevated churn in early tenure (0‚Äì12 months)  
- Higher churn among senior citizens and single customers  
- Lower churn among customers using support and security services  
- Increased churn with manual payment methods  

---

## üß™ Initial Hypotheses Generated
Based on Milestone 1 findings, the following hypotheses are proposed for future testing:
- Customers churn when perceived value does not justify price paid  
- Lack of proactive onboarding increases early-tenure churn  
- Absence of value-added services reduces customer stickiness  
- Manual payment processes increase churn risk  
- Uniform retention strategies fail to address segment-specific churn drivers  

These hypotheses will be **statistically tested and modeled in subsequent milestones**.
